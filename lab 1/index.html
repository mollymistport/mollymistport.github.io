How to open and enjoy a bottle of wine.

By Molly Nance

Images from Unsplash
Step 1: Pick a bottle of wine
Lab 1: Job Aid

In order to open a bottle of wine, you need to choose a bottle of wine! So, either grab a bottle from your collection, or head to the grocery store and pick out a bottle of wine of your liking. New to wine? Read this article for some tips on how to get started. 

Step 2: Choose wine opener

 A wine opener- or corkscrew- is the main tool to open a bottle of wine. If there is foil around the neck of the bottle, take that off with the knife on the corkscrew. Proceed to insert the corkscrew into the cork, and twist down until the screw is fully inside the cork. Believe it or not, there is a variety of wine openers out there! This article from Good Housekeeping provides some insight on the best corkscrews to purchase.

Step 3: Remove cork

Once the corkscrew is in the cork, gently pull the lever upward, and the cork will come out of the bottle. Gently twist the cork clockwise to release from the corkscrew. Keep the cork to use it to close the bottle of wine later.

Step 4: Pour Wine

Pick up the bottle of wine and pour the wine into a glass leaving about 2/3 of the glass empty. This will enable you to swirl the wine around and smell it before tasting. Certain wine glasses are made for certain types of wine. Read this article to learn about which glassware is suitable for the type of wine you picked!  

Step 5: Enjoy your wine!

Swirl the glass of wine and smell it. It should smell of various fruits, minerals and earthy nuances depending on the wine you choose. Then, take one sip of the wine, and swoosh it around your palate. Then take a second sip of wine to fully acclimate your palate to the wine. This is just the beginning of deductive tasting, which we will cover in future job aids. In the meantime, read up on how to store wine properly when opened.
